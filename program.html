Is This Right for Your Child?

This program is a good fit if your child:

  • ✓ Is learning at a level that doesn't always match their classroom or grade
  • ✓ Needs more structure and consistency than traditional tutoring provides
  • ✓ Has gaps or overlaps from changing schools or curricula
  • ✓ Is ready for deeper challenge and wants to understand why the math works
  • ✓ Benefits from being placed based on readiness rather than age or labels

This program may not be the best fit if you're looking for:

  • ✕ Short-term homework help or last-minute support
  • ✕ Test prep or score-focused coaching
  • ✕ Drop-in sessions without ongoing commitment

Our Math Tracks

Placement is based on MAP scores (if available), or a diagnostic assessment and a conversation. I'll recommend the right fit, you don't have to guess.

Core Tracks

Build

Grade 6 topics · RIT 215–229

Ratios, fractions, foundational problem-solving. This is where strong math thinking starts. We build the skills everything else depends on, and we make sure they're solid before moving on.

Momentum

Grade 7 topics · RIT 230–244

Proportional relationships, expressions, equations. Students here are ready to go deeper. We push past procedures and into real understanding, so they're not just getting answers but knowing why.

Ascend

Grade 8–9 topics · RIT 245–259

Linear relationships, functions, algebraic thinking. For students ready to move into high school level math. We make sure they don't just get there, but get there prepared.

Also Available

Foundations

Building core numeracy

Whole number operations, place value, fractions, decimals. For students who need to go back and fill in some gaps before moving forward. We rebuild core numeracy and number sense from the ground up, so the math ahead of them actually makes sense.

Problem Solving

Competition-style challenge

For kids who love math and want to be challenged, like, actually challenged. We're talking non-routine problems, multiple approaches, no hand-holding. Think Art of Problem Solving meets Math Olympiad.

How MAP Fits In

Many international schools use NWEA MAP Growth to understand student readiness across math concepts. We take MAP results seriously, but we don't teach to the test.

MAP helps us see where a student is ready to work. Meaningful growth, however, comes from developing real understanding, not shortcuts or test strategies. Our instruction focuses on strengthening core concepts, reasoning, and mathematical thinking, so progress on MAP is a natural outcome of deeper learning.

We use MAP Growth as one of several inputs to inform placement and track progress over time, alongside classroom performance and teacher observation. The goal is always to place students where the work is productive and confidence can grow.

The goal isn't the score. The goal is the thinking.

Schedule & Time Zones

Classes meet once per week for a 50-minute live session. Each student is placed in a consistent cohort that meets at the same day and time throughout the term.

Sessions are scheduled for after-school hours in both Europe and South America. When enrolling, families select from available time slots that best fit their schedule.

This structure is designed to create a predictable weekly rhythm while fitting naturally alongside a student's regular school day.
